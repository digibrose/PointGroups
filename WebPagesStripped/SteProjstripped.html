Stereographic Projections

In order to make the representation of the different point groups clear there is a system of graphical representation known as stereographic projections. This is simply a method of mapping spherical objects onto a plane. Markers at the edge of the resulting circle would be positioned on the equator of the sphere and point in the middle at the poles. Symmetry elements at the edges of the cirlce therefore represent axes perpendicular to the primary axis which is represented travelling through the poles. Rather than lines travelling through the circle these perpendicular axes are represented by symbols on the edge. Bold line crossing the circle represent mirror planes which are found in the point groups created by diffraction patterns.

The elements again form axes travelling through the central point but in intermediate positions rather than perpendicular to the primary axis.
