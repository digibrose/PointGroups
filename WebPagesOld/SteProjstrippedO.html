Point Groups 3.

Stereographic Projections

In order to make the representation of the different point groups clear there is a system of graphical representation known as stereographic projections. This is simply a method of mapping spherical objects onto a plane. Markers at the edge of the resulting circle would be positioned on the equator of the sphere and point in the middle at the poles. Symmetry elements at the edges of the cirlce therefore represent axes perpendicular to the primary axis which is represented travelling through the poles. Rather than lines traveling through the circle these perpendicular axes are represented by symbols on the edge.

Using this method of projection we can now build the final two point groups observed in crystallography. These are only observed in cubic crystals and are point groups 23 and 432. Alongside the symmetry elements perpendicular to the primary axis we can now see further axes at neither pole nor equator. The elements again form axes travelling through the central point but in intermediate positions rather than perpendicular to the primary axis.
